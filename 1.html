Data Visualizations

Good Example | Sankey Chart

I have always been a big fan of Sankey graphs. They are quite beneficial for datasets where values exist as states transitions to new states, such as statuses of job applications, or in this case: displaying of dating app matches. Not only is it quite easy to follow when a node starts and finishes its progression through states early on, it is also incredibly easy to follow the state of a node through all conditions. This is likely due to the graph's smooth illustrative nature and ability to easily color code.

Bad Example | The Pie Chart

Just because a visualization is used frequently does not imply that the visualization is effective at displaying data. The pie chart itself is a poor way to display information for several reasons. Firstly, it is not easy to compare values unless there is a strict juxtaposition (such as 90% to 10%). In my opinion, pie charts represent a subset of visualizations that are purely used to try to add credibility to presentations through the showing of data, leading many to tune out and ignore the message of the data itself. If one is trying to display the differences between percentages of data, a simple way to increase the immediate understanding of the data would be to substitute another basic chart such as a bar graph, where it is easier to discern relative differences via a horizontal line.
